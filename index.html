Muhammad Ali

About

Muhammad Ali was an American professional boxer and cultural icon.

He is widely regarded as one of the greatest boxers of all time.

Ali won the world heavyweight championship three times and was known for his quick wit and charismatic personality.

He was a political activist and became a symbol of anti-war sentiment during the Vietnam War.

Today, Ali's legacy continues to inspire millions around the world as a symbol of perseverance, courage, and determination.

biography

Born in Louisville, Kentucky, in 1942, Muhammad Ali began boxing at the age of 12 and quickly became a successful amateur boxer.

In 1964, he defeated Sonny Liston to win the world heavyweight championship and become the youngest heavyweight champion in history.

Throughout his career, Ali was involved in several high-profile fights, including the "Fight of the Century" against Joe Frazier and the "Rumble in the Jungle" against George Foreman.

Ali was also a political activist and refused to be drafted into the army during the Vietnam War, for which he was stripped of his boxing title.

After retiring from boxing, Ali continued to be an ambassador for peace and humanitarian causes and remains an inspiration to millions around the world as one of the greatest boxers of all time.
